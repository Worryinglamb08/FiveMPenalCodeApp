(12)18. Immunity

1) At the request of a law enforcement officer and in return for witness testimony, a district attorney may choose to grant a person either a transactional immunity or use and derivative use immunity.
2) A transactional immunity is a type of immunity where a person cannot be charged for a crime revealed in his/her testimony.
3) A use and derivative use immunity is a type of immunity which guarantees that a person's testimony cannot be used against him/her.
